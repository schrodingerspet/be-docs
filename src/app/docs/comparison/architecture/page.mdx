# Approach & Architecture Comparison

Differentiate the philosophical approach between Hassler's empirical testbed and Sophia S's architectural pipeline.

### Explainability vs. Speed

* **Paper 1 (Hassler)** utilized **SHAP** (Shapley Additive Explanations) to retain interpretability, allowing human operators to see exactly which cyber and physical features triggered the alarm.
* **Paper 2 (Sophia S)** utilized **PCA** (Principal Component Analysis) to aggressively squash dimensionality, sacrificing some explainability to guarantee a system latency of under 200 milliseconds.

<Callout type="warning">
**Crucial Limitation:** Remind the reader that neither paper has been tested on a heavy industrial swarm drone, only isolated local systems. Both approaches still require scaling validation before deployment in critical infrastructure.
</Callout>

### Data Transparency vs. Architectural Completeness

* **Paper 1** is heavily empirical. Its greatest strength is the rigorous creation, validation, and publication of a real-world dataset combining asynchronous Wireshark PCAPs with drone telemetry.
* **Paper 2** is heavily architectural. It excels in pipeline design, recognizing that a real-world system needs two distinct paths: a supervised path for immediate classification of known threats, and an unsupervised path to catch zero-day anomalies.
