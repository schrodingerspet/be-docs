# Algorithms: Temporal Correlation vs Shallow Models

Understand why temporal models (1D-CNN) beat shallow models (SVM) for False Data Injection attacks.

<Grid columns={2}>
  <GridCard title="Shallow Models (SVM / FNN)" icon="📉">
    Treats each row of telemetry independently. Struggles to detect slow-drift False Data Injection (FDI) because individual data points look normal in isolation.
  </GridCard>
  <GridCard title="Temporal Models (1D-CNN)" icon="📈">
    Evaluates multiple timesteps to understand the *trajectory* of the drone's behavior. Effectively catches anomalies by looking at the sequence context.
  </GridCard>
</Grid>

<Callout type="insight">
**Why 1D-CNN?** While Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) models also analyze temporal sequences, the 1D-CNN proved to be faster and slightly more accurate for this specific UAV telemetry dataset.
</Callout>

### Model Performance Comparison

The 1D-CNN model achieved the highest performance (**96.13% F1-score**) against complex attacks by exploiting temporal correlations across the data fusion stream.

### Deep Dive: How the Temporal Shift Works

<Accordion title="Understanding the Sliding Window Technique">
To feed data into the 1D-CNN, the authors used a sliding window technique. Instead of feeding row `t`, they feed rows `[t-5, t-4, t-3, t-2, t-1, t]`.

This gives the neural network a "memory" of what just happened. If the drone was flying straight (pitch=0) and suddenly reports a massive pitch angle, the network sees the sudden discontinuity and flags it as a GPS spoofing or sensor spoofing anomaly.
</Accordion>
